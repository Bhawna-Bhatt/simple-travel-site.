Lohaghat - Joy of Uttarakhand

Home Pictures

A picturesque town located on the banks of River Lohawati, Lohaghat is one of the most pristine and unsullied destinations of the Uttarakhand's Kumaon region. The pride and joy of the Champawat district, Lohaghat is steeped in art, royalty and history as it served as the cultural capital of the erstwhile Chand dynasty. Not just history and culture, the town is also a hotspot for several adventure activities, including water sports. There are also a few notable and revered spots around too. The journey to Lohaghat and the destination itself are worth the experience. Lohaghat, cocooned amidst tall deodar trees and snow-capped Himalayan peaks, is a haven for those looking to escape the cacophony of the city and spend some time in the lap of nature. The town attracts visitors with its serene beauty, its rich cultural past and uninterrupted natural vistas. There is something almost mystical and romantic about this particular hill town which makes it a favourite among those in love.

The best time to visit is in summers.The tourist attarctions are Abbott Mount , where you can experience British period Bungalows and even visit the world's second highest cricket pitch.Also, about 9 km away from Lohaghat lies Mayawati Ashram. Perched at an altitude of 1,940 m, this is where Swami Vivekananda, on his spiritual journey, visited in 1898 with his European disciples to establish the 'Advaita Ashram'.
